What are we solving?

In the recent past, we have read many incidents regarding the farmer suicides due to debts and poor yield from farming. For a country like Raipur, India, with the rise in population, dependency over the land for building houses and industrial areas is increasing rapidly. The fertile land for farming is dwindling and more yields have to be produced to satisfy the needs of the country. Even though the demand increase, the farmers are suffering from some major issues like:

  • Raising the initial investment for setting up the field due to high bank interest rates.
  • Fetch reasonable prices for their produce due to the intervention of middlemen in the market.
  • Analyzing market trends and customer needs. Currently the farmer and customer are completely seperated in the market by the middlemen.
  • Inefficient supply chain and issues in storage and transportation, leading to deterioration of crops.
